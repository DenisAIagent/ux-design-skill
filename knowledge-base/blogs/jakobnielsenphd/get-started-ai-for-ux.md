# Getting Started with AI for UX

**Date:** 2023-10-18

**Source:** [Original Article](https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/get-started-ai-for-ux)

---

## Summary

Use generative-AI tools to support and enhance your UX skills -- not to replace them. Start with small UX tasks, and watch out for hallucinations and bad advice.

## Introduction

Jakob Nielsen and Kate Moran co-authored this piece based on crowdsourced advice from over 40 LinkedIn respondents. The article presents current guidance, though specifics will evolve as AI technology advances.

## Why Use AI in UX Work?

### Increased Productivity and Improved Quality

Research demonstrates business professionals work faster with AI assistance. One study found that "consultants at an elite consulting company increased productivity by 33% and rated quality of deliverables by 40% when using AI."

For designers, AI-generated ideation is particularly valuable since the technology can rapidly produce numerous concepts at minimal cost.

### Enhance Your Skills

AI amplifies existing human capabilities rather than replacing them. The technology enables professionals to curate AI-generated options, keeping only the most promising ideas. This human-AI partnership yields superior results than either could achieve independently.

Doris Lin offered an apt metaphor: AI functions as a "sidekick" to UX professionals--accelerating workflows and strengthening outcomes without eliminating the need for human judgment.

Jakob's Second Law of AI states: "You won't lose your job to AI, but to someone who uses AI better than you do."

## AI Is Safest for Experienced UX Professionals

All UX professionals benefit from AI, yet success requires "a heavy dose of human judgment in the workflow" for three critical reasons:

### Humans Must Select Ideas

AI excels at generating endless variations, but not all suggestions prove useful. Senior UX professionals with traditional experience are best equipped to identify valuable concepts and refine raw output.

### Hallucinations Can Be Convincing

AI confidently presents false information as established fact. Its skilled writing makes bad advice appear authoritative rather than obviously flawed. Human review prevents hallucinations from damaging results.

### Bias Lurks in Training Data

AI reflects its training sources, predominantly English-language internet content from Western countries. This creates gaps for teams serving international markets.

## Tips for Junior UXers Getting Started with AI

Newer professionals should experiment with AI while exercising "particular care in judging the output." Recommended approaches include:

- Treat AI as a starting point for learning UX terminology and techniques
- Request sources and links; verify information independently
- Remember AI excels at crafting plausible-sounding responses regardless of accuracy

## How to Use AI

Primary interaction occurs through prompts--written questions or commands clarifying what you need.

### Provide Ample Context

UX professionals famously answer with "It Depends!" because optimal solutions depend heavily on context. Prompts should include details about study type, target audience, budget, and timeline.

### Ask for Multiple Options + Iterate

"The process of using AI is iterative and requires adjustments to fine-tune the outcomes." Techniques like accordion editing and apple picking help refine results:

- **Accordion editing**: Iteratively expand or compress AI-generated text length
- **Apple picking**: Reference elements from previous AI responses to modify subsequent prompts

### Build a Prompt Library

Document exact wording for prompts that work well within your scenarios, saving time and preserving effective alternatives for future reference.

## Specific UX Tasks for AI

**Design:**
- Generate ideas for creative inspiration
- Create illustrations for personas and journey maps
- Plan workshop agendas
- Produce realistic text and images for prototypes

**Research:**
- Write user interview questions
- Conduct sentiment analysis for initial theme discovery
- Analyze user feedback for common pain points
- Rewrite research reports for non-specialist audiences

**Content:**
- Write emails, concepts, or posts from outlines
- Improve UX writing for conciseness and clarity

## What AI Can't Do

### Design Critique Is Problematic

While AI can suggest design improvements, "many of its insights are wrong, even if argued in polished language." Experienced professionals may distinguish valid from hallucinated recommendations, but junior staff should avoid this application.

### User Research Cannot Be Replaced

AI cannot substitute for testing with actual users. The technology mimics "typical" human behaviors but cannot predict how specific user groups with distinct backgrounds, needs, and motivations will truly behave.

Most UX methods require grounding in real data from actual users. AI excels at structuring and interpreting existing data, though interpretations require verification.

## Start Now, Start Small

"You must start now because it requires those few years to become highly qualified and experienced later." Every day without AI practice undermines future competitiveness.

Begin modestly: "Don't try to use AI to solve your biggest UX problems right away. Start with smaller tasks, such as generating user personas or writing microcopy."

Within months, confidence builds for medium-sized projects. Within a year, most UX work can leverage AI assistance. The critical principle: maintain human judgment throughout.
