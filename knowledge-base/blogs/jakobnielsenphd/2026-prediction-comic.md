# 2026 Predictions as a Comic Book and Poster Series

**Date:** 2026-01-26

**Source:** [Original Article](https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/2026-prediction-comic)

---

## Summary

Jakob Nielsen presents his 18 predictions for UX and AI in 2026 through three accessible formats: a 5-minute music video, a 10-page comic strip, and a 10-poster series. All visuals were created using Nano Banana Pro.

## Key Predictions Overview

1. **Accelerating Change:** AI task capabilities doubling every four months
2. **No AGI in 2026:** Superintelligence may emerge around 2030
3. **Possible New Scaling Law:** Breakthroughs remain inherently unpredictable
4. **Temporary Moat:** AI lab advantages last only months
5. **UX as Differentiator:** Foundation models have converged; UX distinguishes products
6. **Google's Integration:** Google will create coherent UX across AI products
7. **Compute Crisis:** Computing remains a bottleneck
8. **AI Agents Rise:** Passive chat shifts to active agentic systems
9. **Generative UI:** Static interfaces become obsolete
10. **Dark Patterns Evolve:** Manipulation moves to persuasive AI systems
11. **Multimodal AI:** Frontier models integrate all modalities as first-class
12. **Single-Mode Consolidation:** Specialized AI companies face acquisition or failure
13. **Image Editing Advances:** Generation shifts to design software with semantic controls
14. **Two-Tier World:** Cognitive class system between premium and free-tier users
15. **Ultimate Niche Targeting:** Individual personalization replaces audience segments
16. **Physical AI Expansion:** Autonomous vehicles and robots proliferate
17. **Apprenticeship Comeback:** Evaluation shifts to decision quality
18. **Human Touch Remains Optional:** Content quality matters more than production method

## Comic Creation Details

Nielsen used character consistency reference sheets to maintain four team members across the 10-page comic. The process required rendering 120 images to produce 10 publishable pages, costing approximately $4. The poster series required 48 renders to select 10 final pieces, costing roughly $1.60.
