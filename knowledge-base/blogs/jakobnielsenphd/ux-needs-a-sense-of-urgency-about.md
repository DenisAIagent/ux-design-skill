# UX Needs a Sense of Urgency About AI

**Date:** 2023-06-14

**Source:** [Original Article](https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/ux-needs-a-sense-of-urgency-about)

---

## Summary

Jakob Nielsen argues that UX professionals must prioritize AI design or risk becoming obsolete, drawing parallels to the dot-com era when UX designers were slow to embrace web design.

## The AI Urgency for UX

Nielsen contends that contemporary UX practitioners mirror the complacency of their 1990s counterparts. Rather than focusing on emerging technologies, many remain absorbed in traditional deliverables and methodologies while "designing yesteryear's UX instead of focusing on next-generation UX."

### Current State of AI Tools

Existing AI-driven tools suffer from poor usability. Despite their inadequacies, these applications already deliver sufficient productivity gains to entice business executives into rapid adoption. However, "engineering-biased and marketing-fueled designs yield subpar user experiences," particularly when user research remains minimal.

Most productivity research on AI tools originates from economists rather than UX professionals, lacking the qualitative insights necessary for superior interface design. Field studies and usability testing conducted by UX experts could significantly improve industry-specific AI applications.

## Historical Parallels

During 1994, web growth reached 1,450%, expanding from 800 to 11,600 websites. Nielsen championed web usability during this period but found himself largely isolated. By the late 1990s, when more designers finally engaged with web projects, advertising designers--not interaction specialists--had already established design conventions, resulting in beautiful yet unusable websites.

## Career Implications

Nielsen projects substantial employment opportunities for UX professionals with two years of AI experience by 2025. With approximately 2,000 UX professionals gaining AI expertise in 2023, but an anticipated million companies needing such specialists by 2025, the supply-demand imbalance creates significant career advantages for pioneers. He suggests "500 job opportunities for each eligible applicant."

## The Windshield Metaphor

Nielsen concludes by referencing his former boss Scott McNealy's observation: professionals must either become "the windshield" mastering emerging technologies or "the bug" rendered obsolete by technological change. Traditional UX skills will remain valuable, but practitioners who leverage AI tools will outpace those maintaining conventional approaches.
