# 10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design

**Author:** Jakob Nielsen
**Published:** April 24, 1994 (Updated January 30, 2024)
**Source:** https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/

## The 10 Heuristics

1. **Visibility of System Status** — Keep users informed through appropriate feedback
2. **Match Between System and Real World** — Speak users' language, not internal jargon
3. **User Control and Freedom** — Provide clearly marked "emergency exits" (undo/redo)
4. **Consistency and Standards** — Follow platform and industry conventions
5. **Error Prevention** — Prevent problems before they occur (slips vs. mistakes)
6. **Recognition Rather than Recall** — Make elements visible, minimize memory load
7. **Flexibility and Efficiency of Use** — Shortcuts for experts, simple for novices
8. **Aesthetic and Minimalist Design** — Focus on essentials, remove irrelevant info
9. **Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors** — Plain language, suggest solutions
10. **Help and Documentation** — Searchable, task-focused, concise

"The 10 heuristics have remained relevant and unchanged since 1994."
